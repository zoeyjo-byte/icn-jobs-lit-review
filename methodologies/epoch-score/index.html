    EPOCH Score Framework¶

    The EPOCH score is a measurement framework developed by MIT researchers Loaiza and Rigobon (2025) that assesses the extent to which occupations rely on human capabilities that are resistant to AI automation and complementary to AI systems.

    Framework Dimensions¶

    EPOCH is an acronym representing five key dimensions of human-intensive capabilities:

    • Empathy: The ability to understand and share the feelings of others, build meaningful connections, and demonstrate compassion
    • Presence: Physical presence, networking abilities, and navigating social contexts to build trust and collaborate effectively
    • Opinion: The capacity to navigate open-ended, ambiguous, or ethical scenarios requiring human judgment and discernment
    • Creativity: Original thinking, improvisation, and the generation of novel ideas and solutions
    • Hope: Grit, perseverance, initiative, and the ability to set strategic direction and inspire others

    Application in AI Research¶

    According to PwC's 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer, the EPOCH framework has been instrumental in understanding how AI reshapes work:

    • New tasks added to AI-exposed roles since 2022 are 2.5x more likely to rely on these human-intensive capabilities
    • The average EPOCH score for new tasks in the most AI-exposed occupations is 0.47, compared to 0.19 for the least exposed
    • Both professionalised roles (0.48) and democratised roles (0.45) show significantly higher EPOCH scores than low exposure roles (0.26)

    Statistical Evidence¶

    The report shows that occupations with higher AI exposure consistently demonstrate higher EPOCH scores, indicating a growing emphasis on human capabilities as AI handles more routine tasks. This pattern is accelerating, with the gap in human-intensive skill requirements widening over time.

    By 2025, the skill transformation gap between high and low AI-exposure occupations reached 116% - meaning the most AI-exposed occupations were evolving their skillsets at more than twice the rate of the least exposed roles.

    Strategic Implications¶

    The EPOCH framework provides valuable insights for both business leaders and workers: - Organizations should invest in developing these human-intensive capabilities alongside AI skills - Workers should focus on honing these skills to remain competitive in an AI-augmented workplace - Educators should incorporate EPOCH dimensions into curriculum design for future workforce readiness
