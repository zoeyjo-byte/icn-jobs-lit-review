## Update: 2026-06-27

PwC's AI Occupational Exposure Index quantifies occupational susceptibility to AI through a multi-dimensional methodology. Ranging 0-1, it analyzes 2,000+ work activities across 800+ occupations, assessing technical feasibility, economic viability, and adoption timelines for AI automation. The 2026 report reveals 74% of jobs have meaningful exposure (quartiles 2-4), with the top quartile experiencing transformative impacts. Figure 3 demonstrates how the index interacts with expertise requirements, showing high-exposure jobs either increase (professionalisation) or decrease (democratization) expertise demands. The methodology underpins critical findings: Figure 7 uses it to segment entry-level job trends, Figure 9 to analyze skill change velocity (top quartile changing 2.2x faster), and Figure 12 to correlate exposure with productivity. Notably, the index evolved to incorporate generative AI capabilities post-2022, explaining accelerated impacts observed after ChatGPT's release. Organizations apply it to identify at-risk roles, prioritize reskilling, and benchmark against industry peers—particularly valuable for navigating the 'superstar effect' where top-quartile firms achieve 163% productivity growth. The report details sector-specific adaptations (e.g., healthcare's focus on diagnostic support roles), making it versatile for workforce planning. When combined with the [[epoch-score]], the index forms the empirical foundation of the [[ai-job-future-framework]], enabling organizations to systematically address all four framework questions. Its longitudinal design (2012-2025 data) provides unprecedented insight into AI's accelerating labor market transformation.
