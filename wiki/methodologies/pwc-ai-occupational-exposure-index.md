## Update 2026-06-26

The PwC AI Occupational Exposure Index, as applied in the 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer, categorizes jobs into quartiles based on AI capability exposure. Figures 7, 9, and 12 rely on this index to analyze trends, showing how the ChatGPT release (late 2022) accelerated divergence between high- and low-exposure roles. The index underpins key findings: flattening of high-exposure [[entry-level-jobs]] (Figure 7), widening skill change gaps (2.2x faster in top quartile, Figure 9), and the [[superstar-effect]] in productivity (Figure 12). Updated annually, the index now incorporates generative AI capabilities, explaining why professionalised roles grew 39% versus 17% for democratised jobs—data critical for the [[ai-job-future-framework]]'s supply-demand analysis.
