# PwC AI Occupational Exposure Index

The PwC AI Occupational Exposure Index is a proprietary methodology developed by PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) to measure the degree to which different occupations are exposed to AI capabilities. This index serves as a foundational tool for PwC's Global AI Jobs Barometer analysis.

## Methodology Overview

The index assesses occupational exposure to AI through a multi-dimensional framework that considers:

1. **Task Analysis**: Evaluation of specific tasks within occupations to determine which could be automated or augmented by current AI capabilities
2. **Skill Mapping**: Analysis of required skills against AI's current capabilities and limitations
3. **Technology Adoption Trends**: Incorporation of real-world AI adoption patterns across industries
4. **Expert Assessment**: Input from industry specialists on the feasibility of AI implementation in different occupational contexts

## Application in Research

The PwC AI Occupational Exposure Index is used to:

- Categorize occupations into quartiles of AI exposure (lowest to highest)
- Identify professionalised vs. democratised jobs based on how AI changes expertise requirements
- Track changes in skill demands across different exposure levels over time
- Analyze company-level performance based on sectoral AI exposure

## Key Insights from the Index

Analysis using the index has revealed several critical patterns:

- **Skill Transformation Acceleration**: The most AI-exposed occupations are evolving their skillsets at more than twice the rate of the least exposed roles, with the gap widening to 116% by 2025
- **Human-Intensive Skill Growth**: New tasks added to highly AI-exposed roles are 2.5x more likely to rely on human-intensive capabilities measured by the EPOCH framework
- **Entry-Level Transformation**: The highest AI exposure quartile of entry-level jobs has flatlined since 2022, while roles that have been "seniorised" are growing at 35%

## Differentiation from Other Measures

Unlike simpler automation risk assessments that focus solely on task replacement, the PwC index captures the nuanced ways AI reshapes work:

- It distinguishes between professionalisation (AI automating less expert tasks) and democratisation (AI automating more expert tasks)
- It tracks how AI exposure affects not just job numbers but also skill requirements, wages, and career progression
- It incorporates both technological feasibility and real-world adoption patterns

## Strategic Value

The index provides valuable insights for multiple stakeholders:

- **Business Leaders**: Helps identify which roles are likely to transform most significantly and where to focus workforce development
- **Workers**: Guides career decisions by highlighting which occupations are gaining or losing value in the AI era
- **Educators**: Informs curriculum development to prepare students for evolving skill requirements
- **Policy Makers**: Supports workforce planning and transition programs
