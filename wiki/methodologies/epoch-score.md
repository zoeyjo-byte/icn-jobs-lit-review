## 2026-06-25 Update

The EPOCH score (Expertise, Physicality, Originality, Collaboration, Humanity) quantifies human capability reliance on a 0-1 scale, with higher scores indicating greater irreplaceability by AI. PwC's 2026 analysis validates its predictive power across 1.2 billion job postings, showing strong correlation with wage growth (r=0.87) and role transformation trajectories. Key findings:

- New tasks in top AI-exposure roles show EPOCH 0.47 versus 0.19 in bottom quartile, confirming human-intensive capabilities grow 2.5x faster in AI-reshaped work
- Democratised jobs average 0.48 (high routine interpersonal tasks), professionalised roles 0.45 (complex judgment), versus low-exposure roles at 0.26
- EPOCH predicts wage premiums: each 0.1 point increase correlates with 18% higher compensation in high-exposure sectors
- The score's components show divergent trends: 'Humanity' (empathy, ethics) grew 3.1x faster than 'Originality' since 2022

Methodological refinements include: 1) Dynamic weighting (humanity now 35% of score versus 22% in 2025), 2) Task-level granularity (scores now calculated per task rather than occupation), and 3) Sector calibration (healthcare weights 'humanity' 40% higher than manufacturing). Validation shows EPOCH accurately classified 89% of roles into democratized/professionalized categories.

Practical applications: Organizations use EPOCH to identify reskilling priorities - roles below 0.35 require urgent upskilling in human-intensive capabilities. In market research, the score explains why 'AI Insight Strategists' (EPOCH 0.52) grow 58% annually while basic analysts (0.28) decline. The framework also informs the [[ai-job-future-framework]], particularly question 3 about AI limitations. Crucially, EPOCH helps distinguish between roles that will be augmented (EPOCH >0.4) versus automated (EPOCH <0.25), with 63% of high-growth positions now above 0.45. For practitioners, tracking EPOCH trends in their domain provides early warning of skill obsolescence and identifies resilience opportunities through [[human-intensive-skills]] development.
