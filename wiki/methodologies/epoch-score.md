The EPOCH score measures the reliance on human capabilities in various occupations, particularly focusing on irreplaceable human skills like empathy, creativity, and face-to-face presence. Higher scores indicate greater dependence on intrinsically human capabilities that are difficult to automate. The methodology assesses occupational task composition to quantify how AI reshapes human work requirements, serving as a key diagnostic tool for identifying roles most resilient to automation and those requiring significant upskilling. It helps distinguish between jobs where AI replaces human tasks versus those where it augments human capabilities.

## Update: 2026-06-25
The PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer (Figures 10-11) applies the EPOCH score to measure human capability reliance in AI-reshaped work. Figure 10 shows new tasks added to AI-exposed roles since 2022 are 2.5x more likely to rely on human-intensive capabilities, with the top AI exposure quartile averaging 0.47 for new tasks versus 0.19 for the bottom quartile.

Figure 11 reveals that in 2025, democratised roles have the highest average EPOCH score (0.48), followed by professionalised roles (0.45), while low-exposure roles score significantly lower (0.26). This counterintuitive pattern—democratised roles showing higher human reliance despite reduced expertise requirements—indicates that both democratization and professionalization pathways increase focus on irreplaceable human skills, albeit through different mechanisms. The data demonstrates the EPOCH score's utility in quantifying how AI reshapes occupational demands toward human-intensive capabilities across all exposure levels, providing critical insights for workforce planning and skills development strategies.
