The EPOCH score measures the reliance on human capabilities in various occupations. It quantifies how much a role depends on inherently human skills that are difficult to automate, such as empathy, creativity, and complex judgment. Higher scores indicate greater human intensity.

## Update 2026-06-25

The [[pwc-2026-global-ai-jobs-barometer]] (Figure 10) reveals critical insights about EPOCH score dynamics: new tasks added to AI-exposed roles since 2022 have an average EPOCH score of **0.47** in the top 25% AI exposure quartile, compared to **0.19** in the bottom quartile—making them **2.5 times more human-intensive**.

Figure 11 shows how EPOCH scores vary by job classification: democratised roles average **0.48**, professionalised roles **0.45**, and low exposure roles **0.26**. This confirms that both AI transformation pathways (democratization and professionalization) increase reliance on human capabilities, though democratised roles show slightly higher human intensity. The data demonstrates EPOCH score's value in tracking how AI reshapes occupational skill requirements.
