## Update 2026-06-26

Figures 10 and 11 in the PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer provide critical new applications of the EPOCH score. Figure 10 shows new tasks in the most AI-exposed occupations have an average EPOCH score of 0.47 (vs 0.19 in least exposed), meaning they are 2.5x more reliant on human capabilities like empathy and creativity. Figure 11 breaks this down by job type: democratised jobs score highest (0.48), followed by professionalised jobs (0.45), while low-exposure jobs score only 0.26. This validates the EPOCH score's utility in measuring human intensity across AI exposure levels and explains why democratised roles—which constitute 52% of jobs—still show strong demand for [[human-intensive-skills]] despite AI automation of expert tasks.
