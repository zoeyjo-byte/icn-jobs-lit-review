The EPOCH score measures the reliance on human capabilities in various occupations.

## Update: 2026-06-27

The EPOCH score, [[pwc]]'s proprietary metric, quantifies occupational reliance on human capabilities difficult for AI to replicate. Ranging 0-1, it evaluates dimensions including empathy, creativity, physical dexterity, and face-to-face interaction through job description analysis and expert assessment. Figure 10 of the 2026 report provides definitive validation: new tasks added to AI-exposed roles since 2022 show EPOCH scores 2.5 times higher in the top exposure quartile (0.47) versus bottom quartile (0.19), proving AI shifts work toward human-intensive activities. Figure 11 further demonstrates its significance by showing democratised roles (0.48) and professionalised roles (0.45) maintain substantially higher scores than low exposure roles (0.26), indicating both AI impact pathways increase human reliance. The methodology explains key economic trends: occupations with higher EPOCH scores in AI-exposed roles command greater wage premiums (Figure 15), as human skills become scarcer value drivers. Crucially, the score helps operationalize the [[ai-job-future-framework]]'s 'AI's limitations' dimension by identifying tasks requiring human oversight. The report shows EPOCH scores are rising fastest in high-exposure roles (Figure 9), with the top quartile's score increasing 116% from 2023-2025. This metric has become essential for organizations mapping future skill requirements, particularly in designing [[professionalised-jobs]] and addressing the human elements of 'superstar' company success (Figure 12). Policymakers also use it to target workforce development initiatives where human capabilities provide sustainable competitive advantages.
