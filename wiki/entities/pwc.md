# PwC

PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) is a multinational professional services network and one of the Big Four accounting firms. It provides audit and assurance, tax, and advisory services to corporations, governments, and non-profit organizations globally. With operations in 157 countries and over 360,000 employees, PwC serves as a leading authority on economic and labor market trends.

## Relevance to AI and Market Research

PwC has emerged as a pivotal publisher of research on artificial intelligence's impact on the global labor market. Its annual **Global AI Jobs Barometer** report analyzes trends using data from over one billion job advertisements, providing critical insights for the market research industry. The firm's proprietary [[pwc-ai-occupational-exposure-index]] methodology has become a standard for measuring AI's workforce impact.

## Key Contributions

- Developed the [[epoch-score]] framework for measuring human capability reliance in occupations
- Identified the dual trends of [[ai-democratization]] (52% of jobs) and [[ai-professionalization]] (22% of jobs)
- Documented the [[superstar-effect]] where top AI-adopting companies achieve disproportionate productivity gains
- Quantified wage premiums for [[ai-skills]] across economic sectors

PwC's research directly informs talent strategy for market research firms navigating AI adoption. Their data provides empirical validation for skills gap analyses and workforce planning.
