# PwC

PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC) is a multinational professional services network and one of the Big Four accounting firms. Founded in 1998 from a merger of Price Waterhouse and Coopers & Lybrand, PwC operates in 155 countries with over 360,000 employees worldwide.

## Role in Market Research

PwC has established itself as a significant contributor to market research, particularly in the intersection of technology, business strategy, and workforce transformation. The firm publishes numerous industry reports and thought leadership pieces that influence business decision-making globally.

## Key Contributions to AI and Workforce Research

PwC is the publisher of the annual Global AI Jobs Barometer, a comprehensive analysis of AI's impact on the labor market. The 2026 edition analyzed over one billion job advertisements across six continents to identify how AI is reshaping work.

The firm also conducts the Global CEO Survey, which provides insights into executive perspectives on AI adoption and business strategy. According to their 2026 survey, 49% of CEOs expect AI adoption to decrease junior hiring in the next three years.

## Research Methodology

PwC's workforce research typically combines:

- Large-scale data analysis (job postings, company performance metrics)
- Proprietary indices (like the AI Occupational Exposure Index)
- Framework development (such as the professionalisation/democratisation lens)
- Cross-referencing with academic research (MIT's EPOCH framework, Teeselink and Carey's expertise analysis)

## Strategic Positioning

PwC positions itself at the intersection of technology implementation and business strategy, helping organizations navigate AI adoption not just for efficiency but for growth and reinvention. Their research consistently emphasizes that the greatest value from AI comes from using it to create new forms of value rather than simply automating existing processes.
