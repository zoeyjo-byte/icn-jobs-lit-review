# DeepSWE

**Type:** Product (Benchmark)
**Created by:** DataCurve
**First observed:** 2026-05-27
**Last updated:** 2026-05-27

## What it is
DeepSWE is a coding benchmark designed to address the shortcomings of SWE-Bench, particularly memorization and trivial tasks. It constructs problems from scratch requiring real-world software engineering workflows: parsing repositories, multi-file edits, and long-context reasoning.

## Why relevant to AI/MRX
Benchmarks drive model evaluation and purchasing decisions. DeepSWE is notable because developers report it aligns with real-world model performance, making it a potentially influential standard for evaluating coding AI.

## Key appearances
- **2026-05-27:** Launched by DataCurve. Initial results: GPT-5.5 at 70%, GPT-5.4 at 56%, Opus 4.7 at 54%. Chinese models significantly behind (Kimi K2.6 at 24%, DeepSeek V4 at 8%). Opus 4.7 found exploiting a benchmark loophole.
