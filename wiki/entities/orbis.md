# ORBIS

ORBIS is a comprehensive global business information database maintained by Bureau van Dijk (a Moody's Analytics company). It provides detailed financial, ownership, and operational data on millions of companies worldwide, serving as a critical resource for business intelligence and economic research.

## Role in AI Research

In PwC's 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer, ORBIS data served as the primary source for company-level analysis, particularly regarding productivity, headcount, and wage growth metrics across different levels of AI exposure.

## Data Coverage

ORBIS includes information on:

- Over 400 million companies globally
- Financial statements and performance metrics
- Ownership structures and corporate hierarchies
- Industry classifications using standard systems like NAICS
- Employment data and executive information

For the PwC analysis, the database provided longitudinal data from 2018-2025 for companies with consistent reporting.

## Application in PwC's Research

PwC used ORBIS data to:

- Measure productivity growth (revenue per employee) across AI exposure levels
- Track headcount changes at companies in different AI exposure quartiles
- Analyze wage growth patterns relative to AI adoption
- Identify the "superstar effect" among highly AI-exposed companies

## Key Insights Enabled by ORBIS Data

Analysis of ORBIS data revealed several critical patterns:

- The most AI-exposed companies showed 33.5% productivity growth (2018-2025) compared to 18.7% for least exposed
- Headcount growth at most AI-exposed companies reached 52.2% versus 23.3% for least exposed
- Wage growth at most AI-exposed companies reached 24.4% versus 5.6% for least exposed
- The top 20% of most AI-exposed companies ("superstars") achieved 163% productivity growth

## Methodological Considerations

PwC's analysis using ORBIS data included several important methodological notes:

- Focus on larger formal firms with annual turnover of at least $50m USD
- Exclusion of companies that exited the market during the period (creating survivorship bias)
- Potential bias toward larger, more formalized companies in some countries
- Company AI exposure determined by NAICS sector classification

These considerations mean the analysis reflects patterns among established businesses rather than the entire economy, but still provides valuable insights into how AI adoption correlates with business performance.
