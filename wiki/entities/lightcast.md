# Lightcast

Lightcast (formerly Emsi Burning Glass) is a labor market analytics company that provides detailed data on job postings, skills demand, and workforce trends. The company aggregates and analyzes millions of job postings from across the internet to create comprehensive labor market intelligence.

## Role in AI Research

Lightcast data serves as a primary source for PwC's Global AI Jobs Barometer analysis. According to the 2026 report, the analysis examined over one billion online job advertisements across six continents, with Lightcast providing the underlying job posting data.

## Data Collection Methodology

Lightcast gathers job posting data through:

- Web scraping of major job boards and company career sites
- Partnerships with job posting aggregators
- Direct feeds from some large employers
- Historical archives dating back to 2012 for certain markets

The company processes this raw data to extract structured information about required skills, qualifications, compensation, and other job characteristics.

## Application in PwC's Research

In the PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer, Lightcast data was used to:

- Track changes in job posting volumes across different AI exposure levels
- Analyze skill requirements and how they evolve over time
- Calculate wage premiums for AI-related skills
- Identify trends in entry-level hiring patterns
- Measure growth in AI specialist positions

## Key Insights Enabled by Lightcast Data

Analysis of Lightcast data revealed several critical patterns:

- AI specialist job postings grew eight times faster than total job postings from 2012-2025
- Entry-level job postings in the highest AI exposure quartile flatlined after 2022
- Skills needed for the most AI-exposed jobs are changing 2.2x faster than for the least exposed jobs
- Wage premiums for AI skills reached 62% across all sectors in 2025

## Limitations and Considerations

While valuable, Lightcast data has certain limitations:

- Primarily captures formal, online job postings (may miss informal hiring)
- Coverage varies by country and time period
- Skill extraction relies on natural language processing of job descriptions
- May not fully capture internal mobility or promotions

Researchers using Lightcast data, like PwC, typically note these limitations and adjust their methodologies accordingly.
