Senior skills traditionally required in more senior positions that are now increasingly appearing in entry-level job descriptions due to AI adoption. This phenomenon reflects the compression of career ladders as AI handles foundational tasks, pushing entry-level roles to demand capabilities previously associated with mid-career professionals.

## Update 2026-06-25

The [[pwc-2026-global-ai-jobs-barometer]] (Figure 8) quantifies this trend in the US market: entry-level job postings that have been "seniorised" (requiring traditionally senior skills) are projected to grow by **35%** between 2019 and 2025, while non-seniorised entry-level job postings are expected to decline by **10%**. This represents a 45 percentage point divergence driven by AI adoption.

This acceleration aligns with Figure 4's finding that professionalised roles demand additional skills at **twice the rate** of democratised roles (68% vs 33% growth from 2018-2025). The data confirms that AI is reshaping entry-level expectations, particularly in high AI-exposure occupations where human judgment becomes critical for overseeing AI outputs.
