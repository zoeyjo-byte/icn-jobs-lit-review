# Human-Intensive Skills

Human-intensive skills are capabilities that are distinctively human and increasingly valued in AI-exposed roles. According to PwC's 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer, new tasks added to AI-exposed roles since 2022 are 2.5x more likely to rely on these human-intensive capabilities compared to tasks in less AI-exposed roles.

## The EPOCH Framework

The EPOCH framework, developed by MIT researchers Loaiza and Rigobon (2025), identifies five key dimensions of human-intensive capabilities that are most resistant to AI automation and complementary to AI systems:

- **Empathy & Emotional Intelligence**: Compassion, understanding, and building meaningful connections with others
- **Presence, Networking & Connectedness**: Physical presence, networking abilities, and navigating social contexts to build trust
- **Opinion, Judgment & Ethics**: Navigating open-ended, ambiguous, or ethical scenarios requiring human discernment
- **Creativity & Imagination**: Original thinking, improvisation, and generating novel ideas
- **Hope, Vision & Leadership**: Grit, perseverance, initiative, and setting strategic direction

## Statistical Evidence

The report shows that the average EPOCH score for new tasks in the most AI-exposed occupations is 0.47, compared to 0.19 for the least exposed occupations - a 2.5x difference. Both professionalised and democratised roles show significantly higher EPOCH scores (0.48 and 0.45 respectively) compared to low exposure roles (0.26).

This trend is accelerating, with the skill transformation gap between high and low AI-exposure occupations widening over time. By 2025, the top quartile of AI-exposed occupations shows 116% higher net skill change compared to the bottom quartile.

## Implications

As AI absorbs routine and technical tasks, these human-intensive skills become increasingly competitively valuable. Workers who develop these capabilities alongside AI proficiency will be best positioned for success in the evolving job market. Organizations should prioritize investing in these skills through training and development programs.
