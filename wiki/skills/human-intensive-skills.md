Human-intensive skills such as empathy, creativity, and face-to-face presence are increasingly important in AI-exposed roles. These non-automatable capabilities represent the growing human edge in an AI-augmented workplace, particularly in customer-facing and collaborative positions. As AI handles routine analytical tasks, the demand for these inherently human skills intensifies across both democratised and professionalised job categories.

## Update 2026-06-25

New data from the [[pwc-2026-global-ai-jobs-barometer]] (Figure 10) reveals that new tasks added to AI-exposed roles since 2022 are **2.5 times more likely** to rely on human-intensive capabilities compared to less exposed roles. Specifically, the [[epoch-score]] for new tasks in the top 25% AI exposure quartile is 0.47, versus 0.19 for the bottom 25% quartile.

Figure 11 further demonstrates that democratised roles (52% of jobs) have an average EPOCH score of 0.48, professionalised roles (22% of jobs) have 0.45, and low exposure roles have 0.26. This confirms that both democratisation and professionalisation pathways increase reliance on human-intensive skills, though through different mechanisms: democratised roles retain less expert human tasks while professionalised roles elevate expertise requirements.
