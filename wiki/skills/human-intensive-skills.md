## Update 2026-06-26

New data from the PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer (Figure 10) reveals that new tasks added to AI-exposed roles since 2022 are 2.5 times more likely to rely on 'human-intensive' capabilities such as empathy, creativity, and face-to-face presence. The EPOCH score (measuring human capability reliance) for the top 25% AI exposure quartile is 0.47, compared to just 0.19 for the bottom 25%. Figure 11 further shows democratised jobs—which constitute 52% of all positions—have the highest average EPOCH score (0.48), indicating these roles increasingly depend on uniquely human skills despite AI automation of expert tasks. This trend explains the growing wage premiums for human-intensive capabilities, particularly in client-facing roles where AI handles routine analysis but humans manage complex interactions and atypical cases.
