## Update: 2026-06-27

Wage premiums for AI skills have risen to 62% (from 57% in 2025), with significant sectoral variation documented in Figure 15 of the PwC report. Consumer Markets leads at 118%, followed by Technology, Media and Telecoms (84%), Energy, Utilities and Resources (75%), Manufacturing (73%), and Professional Services (67%). Financial Services shows 53%, Health Industries 37%, and Government and Public Sector the lowest at 16%. These premiums reflect acute talent shortages in AI implementation roles, particularly for hybrid skills combining technical proficiency (prompt engineering, system integration) with domain expertise. Figure 14 confirms these premiums correlate with productivity gains at AI-exposed companies, where wage growth for AI-skilled workers accelerated to 24.4% in 2025 versus 16.6% for least exposed firms. Crucially, Figure 16 shows AI specialist job postings are growing eight times faster than total postings, with Technology, Media and Telecoms accounting for 11.4% of sector roles by 2025. The report identifies three premium drivers: scarcity of talent capable of overseeing AI quality control (addressing [[ai's limitations]] in the [[ai-job-future-framework]]), demand for skills in atypical case management, and regulatory compliance needs. This underscores AI proficiency as a critical career accelerator, especially in 'superstar' companies achieving 163% productivity growth (Figure 12).
