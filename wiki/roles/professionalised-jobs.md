# Professionalised Jobs

Professionalised jobs are occupations that have been reshaped by AI to demand more human expertise, judgment, and creativity. In these roles, AI automates the relatively basic tasks, leaving humans with more complex and expert responsibilities.

## Definition and Identification

Professionalised jobs are identified through analysis of how AI changes the expertise requirements of occupations. According to PwC's 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer, these jobs represent approximately 22% of advertised positions globally.

A job is classified as professionalised when AI automation leaves humans performing tasks that require more specialized knowledge or capabilities than before AI adoption.

## Examples of Professionalised Jobs

- **Radiologists**: AI handles basic image analysis, leaving humans to interpret complex cases and make diagnostic decisions
- **Employment Recruiters**: AI automatically screens CVs, leaving humans with more demanding tasks like negotiating contracts
- **Air Traffic Controllers**: AI manages routine flight path calculations, leaving humans to handle exceptional situations
- **Financial Analysts**: AI performs data gathering and basic modeling, leaving humans to develop strategic insights
- **Nursing Aides**: AI tracks vital signs and schedules medication, enabling more time for patient relationship building

## Statistical Evidence

Professionalised jobs show distinct advantages in the evolving labor market:

- **Job Growth**: 39% growth in job postings from 2018-2025, more than twice the rate of democratized jobs (17%)
- **Skill Demand**: 68% growth in required skills from 2018-2025, double the rate of democratized jobs (33%)
- **Wage Growth**: 37% growth in advertised salaries since 2021, 42% higher than democratized jobs (26%)

## Impact on Workforce Development

Professionalised jobs are increasingly demanding human-intensive capabilities as outlined in the EPOCH framework. The average EPOCH score for professionalised roles is 0.45, significantly higher than low exposure roles (0.26).

Workers in professionalised roles need to develop complementary skills that become more valuable as AI handles routine components of the job, particularly in areas of judgment, creativity, and strategic thinking.

## Strategic Considerations

Businesses should consider deeper investment in advanced AI fluency, human-intensive skills development, and retention strategies for professionalised roles as demand and wage pressure rises. Workers in these roles should focus on developing expertise that complements rather than competes with AI capabilities.
