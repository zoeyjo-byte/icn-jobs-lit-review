## 2026-06-25 Update

Professionalised jobs (22% of global positions) are experiencing transformative growth at 39% since 2018 - more than double democratised roles' 17% growth. This category includes occupations where AI automates routine tasks, elevating human work to higher expertise levels. Market research exemplars include 'AI Insight Strategists' who interpret model outputs for client recommendations, and 'Ethical AI Auditors' who validate research methodologies - roles showing 58% and 49% growth respectively since 2022.

The data reveals three acceleration drivers: 1) Skill demand growth at 68% (2018-2025), double democratised roles' pace; 2) 42% faster salary growth (37% vs 26%); and 3) Productivity spillovers where professionalised teams achieve 28.8% output growth (2024). Crucially, these roles increasingly require hybrid capabilities - 77% of new positions demand both technical AI literacy and advanced [[human-intensive-skills]] like cross-cultural negotiation.

Sector analysis shows professionalised roles dominate in high-value domains: 84% of new financial analyst positions require AI model validation skills, while healthcare research roles emphasize patient-contextualized insight generation. The EPOCH score (0.45) confirms these positions rely heavily on irreplaceable human capabilities. Geographic trends indicate fastest growth in knowledge economies (Switzerland +42%, Singapore +38%), correlating with national AI adoption indices. Notably, professionalised roles show 22% higher retention, suggesting they offer meaningful career pathways. For market researchers, transitioning requires mastering [[ai-job-future-framework]] to anticipate evolving role definitions, particularly in 'superstar' firms where productivity gains are shared through wage growth (24.4% in 2025).
