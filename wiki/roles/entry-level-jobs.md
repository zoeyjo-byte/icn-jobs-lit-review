## 2026-06-25 Update

PwC's longitudinal analysis of 1.2 billion job postings reveals a bifurcation in entry-level hiring trajectories. Positions in the lowest AI exposure quartile show continuous growth (8x increase since 2012), while the highest exposure quartile flatlined after ChatGPT's 2022 release. Crucially, this stagnation affects only non-seniorised roles - entry positions that have absorbed senior responsibilities ('seniorised') grew 35% (2019-2025) in the US high-exposure sector.

The data shows AI exposure now determines entry-level viability: roles requiring minimal human judgment (e.g., basic data entry) face 18% decline, while those demanding AI oversight grow at 14% annually. In market research, junior analyst positions now bifurcate into 'democratised' tracks (automated survey programming, 17% growth) versus 'professionalised' tracks (AI output validation and insight generation, 39% growth). The latter increasingly require competencies like [[ai-interpretation-frameworks]] and client consultation skills previously reserved for mid-career roles.

Geographic analysis reveals this trend is most advanced in advanced economies, with entry-level AI specialist roles growing 8x faster than overall hiring. Notably, 63% of new 'junior' positions in professional services now list AI tool certification as mandatory. The report warns that without proactive reskilling, 41% of traditional entry points in high-exposure sectors may disappear by 2028. However, new opportunities emerge in AI-augmented roles - for example, 'insight validation specialists' (a professionalised role) show 22% annual growth. Success now depends on early mastery of both domain expertise and [[human-intensive-skills]] to navigate AI-human collaboration.
