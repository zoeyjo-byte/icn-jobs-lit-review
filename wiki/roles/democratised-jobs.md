# Democratised Jobs

Democratised jobs are occupations that have been reshaped by AI to demand less human expertise. In these roles, AI automates the relatively more expert tasks, leaving humans with less demanding responsibilities.

## Definition and Identification

Democratised jobs are identified through analysis of how AI changes the expertise requirements of occupations. According to PwC's 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer, these jobs represent approximately 52% of advertised positions globally.

A job is classified as democratised when AI automation leaves humans performing tasks that require less specialized knowledge or capabilities than before AI adoption.

## Examples of Democratised Jobs

- **Inventory Clerks**: AI now performs complex tasks like managing inventory, leaving humans with less expert tasks like moving stock
- **Software Developers**: AI handles sophisticated coding tasks, leaving humans with more routine implementation work
- **Loan Officers**: AI automates complex risk assessment, leaving humans with more standardized processing tasks
- **Finance Managers**: AI performs advanced analytics, leaving humans with more execution-focused tasks
- **Medical Secretaries**: AI handles scheduling and basic communications, leaving humans with more routine administrative work

## Statistical Evidence

Democratised jobs show distinct patterns in the evolving labor market:

- **Job Growth**: 17% growth in job postings from 2018-2025, less than half the rate of professionalised jobs (39%)
- **Skill Demand**: 33% growth in required skills from 2018-2025, half the rate of professionalised jobs (68%)
- **Wage Growth**: 26% growth in advertised salaries since 2021, 42% lower than professionalised jobs (37%)

## Impact on Entry-Level Opportunities

Democratisation has particular implications for entry-level positions. As David Autor argues, when technology reduces the expertise required for a job, the pool of qualified candidates expands, which can enable the occupation to grow while wages may stagnate.

For example, child care services managers (a democratised role) have seen job listings more than double since 2019 (111% growth) while wages have grown only 8%.

## Strategic Considerations

Businesses should consider proactive work redesign, mobility pathways, and refreshed career propositions for democratised roles as AI changes the expertise required. Workers in these roles may need to focus on developing complementary human-intensive skills to remain competitive or consider transitioning to roles where AI creates rather than reduces expertise requirements.
