Democratised jobs are occupations reshaped by AI to demand less human expertise, representing 52% of advertised positions globally. In these roles, AI automates more expert tasks (e.g., complex analysis), leaving less expert, routine work for humans (e.g., basic data entry). This pathway makes certain occupations more accessible to workers with lower formal training but risks deskilling roles and reducing wage premiums. Democratised jobs are common in administrative, customer service, and operational roles where AI handles cognitive complexity while humans manage execution. While forming the largest job category, they show slower growth metrics compared to professionalised roles.

## Update: 2026-06-25
The PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer (Figures 2, 4-5) quantifies democratised job dynamics. Figure 2 establishes they represent 52% of global jobs, with examples including Human resource managers and Software developers where AI automates complex tasks (e.g., contract negotiation, coding) while leaving routine components.

Figure 4 shows democratised roles have experienced 33% growth in skills demanded (2018-2025)—half the rate of professionalised roles (68%). Figure 5 reveals 17% job posting growth (less than half of professionalised roles' 39%) and 26% salary growth since 2021 (42% slower than professionalised roles). Despite being the majority category, democratised roles show slower transformation and growth metrics. Figure 11 indicates they have the highest average [[epoch-score]] (0.48), confirming increased reliance on human-intensive capabilities even as expertise requirements decrease. This suggests democratised roles are evolving toward human-AI collaboration models where workers focus on execution and oversight of AI outputs, creating accessible but lower-growth career pathways.
