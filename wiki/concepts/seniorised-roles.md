## Update 2026-06-26

Figure 8 in the PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer quantifies seniorised roles in the US: entry-level job postings requiring traditionally senior skills are projected to grow 35% between 2019-2025, while non-seniorised roles decline 10%. This trend is concentrated in the top AI exposure quartile, where AI adoption drives demand for strategic capabilities like contract negotiation (previously reserved for senior recruiters) even at junior levels. The report attributes this to AI handling routine tasks, pushing human workers toward complex activities documented in [[human-intensive-skills]]. Figure 4 shows professionalised roles—which often exhibit seniorisation—demand skills at twice the rate of democratised jobs, with the gap accelerating since 2022 as AI capabilities advance.
