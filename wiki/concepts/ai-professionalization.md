AI professionalization refers to the process where AI automates less expert tasks, leaving more expert tasks for humans. This affects 22% of global jobs, creating roles that demand higher levels of human expertise as AI handles routine components. Professionalised jobs represent the "high-skill" pathway of AI's workforce transformation.

## Update 2026-06-25

New data from the [[pwc-2026-global-ai-jobs-barometer]] (Figure 4) shows professionalised roles demanding additional skills at **twice the rate** of democratised roles (68% vs 33% growth from 2018-2025), with the gap widening significantly since 2022. Figure 5 reveals corresponding wage effects: professionalised roles have seen **42% faster growth** in average salaries relative to democratised roles (37% vs 26% growth since 2021).

Job growth metrics (Figure 5) confirm professionalised jobs are expanding at more than twice the rate of democratised jobs (39% vs 17% growth from 2018-2025). This triple advantage—faster skill demand growth, higher wage growth, and stronger job expansion—positions professionalised roles as the most promising career pathway in the AI-transformed labor market.
