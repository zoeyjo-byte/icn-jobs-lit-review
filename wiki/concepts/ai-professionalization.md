## Update 2026-06-26

Figure 2 in the PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer defines AI professionalization as the process where AI automates less expert tasks (e.g., stock movement for clerks or routine analysis for engineers), leaving more expert work for humans, representing 22% of global jobs. Figure 3 shows this impact is pronounced for high-exposure roles like pharmacists and environmental engineers, where expertise requirements rise up to 20%. Figure 4 demonstrates these roles demand skills at twice the rate of democratised jobs (68% vs 33% growth since 2018), while Figure 5 confirms 39% job growth and 37% salary growth since 2021. This trend underpins the widening skills gap documented in [[human-intensive-skills]] and explains why professionalised roles now dominate AI-driven productivity gains.
