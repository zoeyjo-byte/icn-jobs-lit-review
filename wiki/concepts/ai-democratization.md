AI democratization refers to the process where artificial intelligence automates more expert tasks, leaving less expert tasks for humans. This phenomenon makes certain occupations more accessible to workers with lower levels of formal expertise, as AI handles the complex components of the job. For instance, in market research, AI tools can automate data analysis, enabling junior staff to focus on client communication and interpretation. However, this shift also risks deskilling certain roles and may reduce wage premiums for tasks that become automated. The trend is particularly evident in sectors with high AI adoption, where routine cognitive work is increasingly handled by algorithms. This concept is the counterpart to [[ai-professionalization]], which describes the opposite effect.

## Update: 2026-06-25
New data from the PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer (Figures 2-5) provides detailed empirical evidence. The report states that 52% of jobs globally are being democratized by AI. Figure 3 illustrates this impact across occupations, showing that high-AI-exposure democratized roles—including Human resource managers, Archivists and curators, Credit and loans officers, Commercial sales representatives, Software developers, Systems administrators, Medical secretaries, and Finance managers—experience reduced expertise requirements. For example, AI automates complex contract negotiation for Recruiters, leaving routine CV screening.

Figure 4 reveals democratized roles have seen 33% growth in skills demanded (2018-2025), half the rate of professionalized roles (68% growth). Figure 5 shows democratized roles experienced 26% salary growth since 2021 (42% slower than professionalized roles' 37%) and 17% job posting growth (less than half of professionalized roles' 39%). Despite representing the majority of jobs, democratized roles show slower transformation metrics. This bifurcation highlights how AI creates two distinct labor market pathways, with democratized roles forming the larger but lower-growth segment of the AI-reshaped economy.
