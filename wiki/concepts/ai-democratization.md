## Update 2026-06-26

Figure 2 in the PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer defines AI democratization as the process where AI automates more expert tasks (e.g., inventory management for clerks or CV screening for recruiters), leaving less expert tasks for humans, and accounts for 52% of global jobs. Figure 3 illustrates this impact is strongest for high-AI-exposure democratised roles, where expertise requirements drop up to 60%. Figure 11 shows these roles have the highest average EPOCH score (0.48), indicating persistent reliance on human capabilities despite automation. This trend drives the 35% growth in "seniorised" [[entry-level-jobs]] as AI handles routine work, pushing humans toward client-facing tasks requiring empathy and adaptability—key components of the [[ai-job-future-framework]].
