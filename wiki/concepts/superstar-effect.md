## 2026-06-25 Update

PwC's analysis identifies a pronounced 'superstar effect' where the top 20% of AI-exposed companies achieve dramatically higher returns. These firms show 163% average productivity growth (2025) versus 33.5% for all high-exposure companies, creating a 5x performance gap. The effect accelerates annually - from 3.1x in 2022 to 5.0x in 2025 - driven by three compounding advantages: 1) Talent density (superstars hire 8.2x more AI specialists), 2) Process integration (AI embedded in 92% of workflows versus 47% industry-wide), and 3) Value capture (68% of productivity gains shared as wages versus 24% elsewhere).

New data reveals superstar characteristics: they prioritize professionalised roles (39% growth) over democratised (17%), invest heavily in [[human-intensive-skills]] training (73% of workforce), and use frameworks like [[ai-job-future-framework]] to redesign jobs. Crucially, they convert AI capabilities into revenue growth - consumer-facing superstars show 118% wage premiums for AI skills versus 16% in laggards. The headcount growth gap widens annually (52.2% vs 35.7% in 2025), indicating strategic talent investment.

Sector analysis shows superstars emerge fastest in technology (11.4% AI specialist density) and consumer markets (118% skill premiums), but the effect spans industries. Geographic patterns reveal concentration in innovation hubs (Silicon Valley, Zurich, Singapore), though emerging economy superstars (e.g., Bangalore tech firms) are rising. The report warns that without deliberate intervention, the superstar gap could widen to 8x by 2028, exacerbating inequality.

For market researchers, superstar firms redefine best practices: they treat AI as a co-pilot requiring human oversight, not a replacement. Their success hinges on answering the fourth [[ai-job-future-framework]] question about environmental constraints - for example, navigating GDPR in EU market research. The data suggests non-superstars can close the gap by adopting superstar job redesign principles, particularly in creating hybrid roles that combine AI efficiency with irreplaceable human judgment.
