The superstar effect describes how the top 20% of most AI-exposed companies achieve significantly higher productivity growth rates compared to industry peers. These 'superstar' firms leverage AI not just for automation but for strategic innovation, capturing disproportionate economic benefits through network effects, data advantages, and first-mover advantages in AI implementation. This phenomenon accelerates market concentration as leading companies pull away from competitors, potentially creating winner-takes-all dynamics in AI-adopting sectors. The effect has implications for labor markets, as superstar firms often drive wage growth and set new standards for AI-augmented work.
