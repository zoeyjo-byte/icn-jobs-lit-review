## Update 2026-06-26

Figure 12 in the PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer quantifies the superstar effect: the top 20% of most AI-exposed companies achieve 163% average productivity growth by 2025—five times higher than the 33.5% growth of the most exposed quartile as a whole. This gap widened annually since 2022, with superstar firms outperforming peers by 3x in 2025 (33.5% vs 24.0% for least exposed). Figure 13 shows these companies also lead in headcount growth (52.2% vs 35.7%), while Figure 14 confirms they share gains through higher wage growth (24.4% vs 16.6%). This effect stems from strategic AI implementation using frameworks like the [[ai-job-future-framework]], enabling disproportionate returns from [[ai-specialist-roles]] and human-AI collaboration.
