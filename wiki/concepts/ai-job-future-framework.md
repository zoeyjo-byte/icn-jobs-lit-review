A framework of four questions to analyze how AI is reshaping specific occupations, developed by PwC in their 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer.

## Update: 2026-06-27

The AI Job Future Framework, introduced in Figure 6 of PwC's 2026 report, provides a structured methodology to assess AI's occupational impact through four critical questions: (1) **Expertise**: How is AI changing required human expertise? (e.g., democratization reduces expertise needs while professionalization increases them); (2) **Supply and Demand**: How might job demand and worker supply shift as AI reshapes roles?; (3) **AI's limitations**: Where is human oversight needed for quality control or atypical cases?; and (4) **Environmental forces**: How do regulations, business bottlenecks, or ethics constrain AI adoption? The report demonstrates the framework's application across diverse occupations: For [[pharmacists]], AI handles prescription processing (Expertise shift) but humans manage patient counseling (AI's limitations); for [[software-developers]], AI generates code but humans focus on architecture (Environmental forces). Figure 9 validates the framework by showing how high AI exposure roles undergo 2.2x faster skill changes, directly addressing the Supply and Demand dimension. Organizations using this tool can anticipate skill shifts, redesign roles, and develop targeted training—particularly valuable for navigating the 'superstar effect' (Figure 12). The framework also explains wage dynamics: roles requiring human oversight of AI (Question 3) command higher premiums, as seen in Figure 15's 62% average AI skill premium. By systematically addressing these questions, employers can transform AI disruption into opportunity, while workers identify future-proof career paths in the evolving labor market. This methodology has become essential for strategic workforce planning in the AI era.
