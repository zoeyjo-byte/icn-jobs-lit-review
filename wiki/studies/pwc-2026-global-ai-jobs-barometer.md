## Update 2026-06-26

This update incorporates critical findings from the report's figures:
- **Figure 6** introduces the [[ai-job-future-framework]], a four-question tool for analyzing job evolution across expertise, supply-demand, AI limitations, and environmental forces.
- **Figure 12** reveals the [[superstar-effect]]: top 20% AI-exposed companies achieve 163% productivity growth (vs 33.5% for the exposed quartile), with gaps widening annually since 2022.
- **Figure 15** details sector-specific AI skill wage premiums: 118% in Consumer Markets, 84% in Technology, and 16% in Government.
- **Figure 16** shows AI specialist hiring grew eight times faster than total jobs, with Technology, Media and Telecoms accounting for 11.4% of roles globally by 2025.

The report analyzes over one billion job postings across 20 advanced economies (2012-2025), documenting how AI exposure quartiles drive divergent outcomes in skill demand, wage growth, and job evolution—particularly the 52% of democratised jobs and 22% of professionalised jobs reshaping the labor market.
