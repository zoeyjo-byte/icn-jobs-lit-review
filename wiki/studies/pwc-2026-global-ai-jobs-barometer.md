## 2026-06-25 Update

PwC's 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer, analyzing 1.2 billion job postings across 25 countries, reveals four transformative trends reshaping labor markets. The report introduces the AI Job Future Framework and EPOCH score methodology, validated against longitudinal employment data. Key findings:

**Two-Track Market Emergence**: 52% of jobs are democratized (AI automates expert tasks), 22% professionalized (AI automates routine work), with starkly different trajectories. Professionalised roles grow 39% (2018-2025) versus 17% for democratized, with 42% faster salary growth. Entry-level positions bifurcate - high-exposure roles flatlined since ChatGPT's 2022 release unless 'seniorised' (35% growth for seniorised vs -10% for non-seniorised).

**Superstar Effect**: Top 20% of AI-exposed companies achieve 163% productivity growth (2025) versus 33.5% industry-wide, with gaps widening annually. These firms prioritize professionalised roles, invest in human-AI collaboration, and share 68% of gains as wages (24.4% growth).

**Skills Earthquake**: AI-exposed roles show 2.2x faster skill transformation, with new tasks 2.5x more human-intensive (EPOCH 0.47 vs 0.19). Wage premiums for AI skills hit 62% average (118% in consumer markets), but hybrid skills combining AI literacy with [[human-intensive-skills]] yield 92% premiums in healthcare.

**Investment Acceleration**: AI specialist hiring grew 8x faster than overall jobs, reaching 11.0 (2012=0) by 2025. Sector penetration is universal, led by technology (11.4% of AI roles), with 38% in domain-specialized positions like market research architects.

The report warns of growing inequality without intervention but identifies pathways: organizations using the AI Job Future Framework achieve 28.8% productivity growth, while workers developing skills addressing AI's limitations show 22% higher retention. For market research specifically, the data shows professionalised roles (e.g., AI Insight Strategists) growing 58% annually by focusing on human oversight of AI outputs, while democratised roles (e.g., AI Survey Technicians) grow at 24% through efficiency gains. The Barometer concludes that human capabilities are becoming the critical differentiator in AI-augmented work, with EPOCH scores above 0.45 correlating with sustainable career growth.
