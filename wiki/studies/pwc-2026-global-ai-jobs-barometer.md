# PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer

The PwC 2026 Global AI Jobs Barometer is a comprehensive analysis of over one billion online job advertisements across six continents, examining how AI is reshaping the global labor market. Published by PricewaterhouseCoopers (PwC), this annual report provides critical insights into AI's impact on jobs, skills, and business performance.

## Key Findings

### Two-Track Labor Market
The report identifies a fundamental divergence in how AI affects different types of jobs:
- **Professionalised jobs** (22% of jobs): AI automates less expert tasks, leaving more expert tasks for humans
- **Democratised jobs** (52% of jobs): AI automates more expert tasks, leaving less expert tasks for humans
- **Low AI exposure jobs** (26% of jobs): Jobs with few tasks where AI has capabilities

Professionalised jobs are growing twice as fast as democratised jobs (39% vs. 17% growth from 2018-2025) and seeing 42% higher wage growth since 2021.

### Entry-Level Transformation
The report documents how AI is reshaping entry-level opportunities:
- Entry-level jobs in the highest AI exposure quartile have flatlined since 2022
- AI-exposed entry-level roles are rapidly evolving to require traditionally senior capabilities
- "Seniorised" entry-level roles (requiring more than 10 new senior skills) are growing at 35%, while non-seniorised roles are declining by 10%

### Skills Transformation
The analysis reveals accelerating skill changes:
- Skills needed for the most AI-exposed jobs are changing 2.2x faster than for the least exposed jobs
- New tasks added to AI-exposed roles are 2.5x more likely to rely on human-intensive capabilities
- The skill transformation gap is widening, reaching 116% by 2025

### Superstar Effect
The report identifies a pronounced "superstar effect" among companies:
- The most AI-exposed companies show 33.5% productivity growth (2018-2025)
- The top 20% of these companies ("superstars") achieve 163% productivity growth
- Superstar companies also show higher wage growth (68% vs. 24.4% for all highly exposed companies)

## Methodology

The Barometer combines multiple data sources and analytical approaches:

- **Job Posting Analysis**: Analysis of over one billion job advertisements from Lightcast
- **AI Exposure Index**: PwC's proprietary measure of occupational AI exposure
- **EPOCH Framework**: MIT-developed measurement of human-intensive capabilities
- **Company-Level Analysis**: ORBIS data on productivity, headcount, and wages

The report tracks changes from 2012-2025, with particular focus on the period since 2022 when generative AI adoption accelerated.

## Strategic Implications

For business leaders, the report recommends:
1. Using AI to pursue growth opportunities rather than just efficiency
2. Shaping workforce strategy using the professionalised/democratised lens
3. Investing in agentic AI as the ultimate complement to human expertise
4. Reinventing early career pathways for the AI era
5. Building human-intensive skills alongside AI proficiency

For workers, key recommendations include:
1. Moving toward roles made more expert by AI
2. Seeking opportunities in pioneering companies and industries
3. Learning to command AI as a tool and partner
4. Building human-intensive skills like creativity and judgment
5. Developing senior skills rapidly for early-career workers
